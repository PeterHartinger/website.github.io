Flight Logbook Project Details

Note: All documents referenced are in the German language.

All contributors are anonymised and referenced as participants.

Our client (Professor) wanted a digital version of his flight logbook. To make this happen, we got together as a team of five. At the start of the project, we had a chat with our customer to find out what he was looking for. Based on this info, we put together a specification sheet. At the same time, we put together a requirement sheet. We did this because our professor wanted us to get to grips with both types.

Once the customer had looked over and made some changes to our documents, we carried on. We put together a traceability document. Once we'd got the basics down, we put together a software configuration management plan. We were ready to get started with the coding. We went with a model-view-controller concept.

Once we'd finished building the app, the only thing left to do was to test it. So we put together a test plan. Once we'd run the tests and analysed the results, we were able to put together a test report. The last thing we did was create a release note. We only made one version of this, as we'd already finished the project as part of our studies and there was no point in continuing it.

Finally, we gave our customer a presentation of our product.
